Introduction

In this course students will creatively use algorithms to generate designed form. Generative design - an iterative creative process of ideation, computational form generation, and critique - can be used to analyze and test alternative forms or build upon past forms. Students will explore such alternative computational modes of creativity and learn how to generate and build complex architectonic forms. In a series of charrettes they will design and fabricate an art installation.

Grading
  • Attendance: 10%
  • Videos: 10%
  • Renderings: 10%
  • Fabrication: 10%
  • Project presentation: 10%
  • Installation: 20%
  • Project: 30%

Project

Design and fabricate a procedurally modeled wall installation to be displayed in Brooks Hall.

Requirements
  • A 1 square meter wall installation
  • An ISO A0 board showing your concept, process, and renderings

Syllabus

  • Rhino

    A short introduction to basic modeling techniques in Rhinoceros3D.


    Topics: curves, curve networks, patching, lofting, and extrusion


    Assignments
    • Shop training in the Materials Lab
    • Tutorials on lynda.com

  • Python

    Hello World! An short introduction to programming with Python exploring basic syntax, functions, operators, and logic.


    Topics: variables, lists, loops, nesting, branching, and iteration


    Assignments
    • Tutorials on codecademy

  • Grasshopper

    An introduction to visual programming with Grasshopper. Explore folding as a design theme to learn to think algorithmically. Follow patterns to fold complex forms with paper. Use Grasshopper to procedurally model the same patterns as nurbs surfaces.


    Topics: folding


    Assignments
    • Tutorials on lynda.com and grasshopper.org

  • Generative modeling

    Procedurally model trigonometric curves and surfaces using RhinoPython and Grasshopper and then render your models with the Rhino Renderer and Neon.


    Topics: procedural curves and surfaces


    Assignments
    • Tutorials
    • Fabrication: CNC route a procedurally modeled surface

  • Parametric modeling

    Procedurally model surfaces from mathematically derived arrays using RhinoPython and Grasshopper and then use the model parameters to transform the surfaces.


    Topics: arrays and parameters


    Assignments
    • Tutorials
    • Rendering: render a parametric surface
    • Fabrication: CNC route a parametric surface

  • Randomess

    Explore how randomness can play a generative role in the creative process. Use randomness, noise, and gaussian distributions to model surfaces. Render your models with Keystone.


    Topics: arrays and parameters


    Assignments
    • Project proposal: prepare a presentation to explain your concept and workflow for a final project
    • Rendering: render a random, noisy, or gaussian surface with Keystone
    • Fabrication: CNC route a random, noisy, or gaussian surface with Keystone

  • Concept design

    Begin to design your final project. Refine your concept and workflow during desk critiques.



  • Schematic design

    Develop your design. Refine your process during desk critiques.



  • Detailed design

    Finalize your design. Develop your plans for rendering and fabrication during desk critiques.


    Assignments
    • Fabrication: build your final project

  • Installation

    Install your final project.



  • Reception

    A gallery reception to showcase your hard work.
